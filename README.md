### Problem Statement: The Challenge of Post-SSLC Selection
[cite_start]Students completing their SSLC (Secondary School Leaving Certificate) frequently make suboptimal choices regarding their academic stream or degree path due to a lack of comprehensive awareness about the available options[cite: 3, 4, 5]. [cite_start]They often gravitate towards popular or familiar choices rather than those best suited to their aptitudes and interests, which can significantly impact their future academic and career trajectories[cite: 6, 7].

Existing solutions for career guidance face several limitations:

| Solution | Problem/Limitation |
| :--- | :--- |
| [cite_start]**Career Counselling Platforms** [cite: 9] | [cite_start]They charge fees for service, making them unaffordable for everyone[cite: 10, 11]. |

| [cite_start]**Using the Internet (Google, ChatGPT, Bard.ai)** [cite: 15] | [cite_start]Google is often unhelpful [cite: 17][cite_start], AI applications are trained on specific data [cite: 18][cite_start], and many people lack the skill to effectively use the internet for this purpose[cite: 19]. |

### Proposed Solution: Career Path Finder
[cite_start]The Career Path Finder application is designed to act as a **personalized career compass** to solve the post-SSLC decision-making challenge[cite: 22, 24]. [cite_start] It provides students with the clarity and insight needed to confidently choose their next academic steps[cite: 22, 24].

#### [cite_start]How the Application Empowers Students [cite: 26]

1.  **Comprehensive Exploration of All Options:**
    * [cite_start]The application addresses the issue of limited student awareness by providing a **holistic view** of every viable academic and career option available after SSLC[cite: 28, 29].
2.  **Identifying the Shortest and Most Efficient Paths:**
    * [cite_start]It helps students by illustrating typical academic progressions (Mapping Progression)[cite: 31, 32, 33]. [cite_start]This shows how a specific stream or diploma can lead to an undergraduate degree and, eventually, to a desired career goal[cite: 33].
3.  **Personalized Matching Based on Interests and Aptitudes:**
    * [cite_start]To ensure long-term career satisfaction [cite: 35][cite_start], the application uses **Interest-Based Filtering**[cite: 37].
    * [cite_start]Students can input their passions, favorite subjects, hobbies, and personality traits[cite: 37].
    * [cite_start]The application then filters and prioritizes options that align with these inputs[cite: 38].
